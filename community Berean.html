What is Theology and Why Study It?

Many people feel apprehensive when they hear the word "theology." They imagine complex concepts and inaccessible language. But theology, in its essence, is simply the study of God and divine matters.

At Berean Community, we believe that theological study should be accessible to all Christians, regardless of their academic background. Our method simplifies theological concepts, making them understandable and applicable to daily life.

The secret to healthy theology is learning the fundamentals clearly, correctly, and consistently - and that's exactly what we offer in our courses.

Introduction to Theology

Professor: Teacher Lemos

Duration: 6 weeks

Level: Beginner

Description: This module presents the foundations of Christian theology, exploring its nature, methods, and main divisions. Students will be introduced to basic concepts that will serve as the foundation for subsequent theological studies.

Syllabus:

  • What is theology and why study it
  • Divisions of systematic theology
  • Sources of theology: Bible, tradition, reason, and experience
  • Brief history of theological thought
  • Theology and practical Christian life
  • Basic tools for theological study

Bibliology

Professor: Lemos

Duration: 8 weeks

Level: Intermediate

Description: This course explores the doctrine of Scripture, covering inspiration, authority, inerrancy, canonicity, and interpretation principles.

Syllabus:

  • Nature and inspiration of Scripture
  • Authority and inerrancy of the Bible
  • Formation of the biblical canon
  • Transmission and translation of biblical texts
  • Principles of biblical interpretation
  • Application of Scripture to contemporary life
